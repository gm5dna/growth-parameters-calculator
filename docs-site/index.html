Measurements Guide

Weight

Units: Kilograms (kg)

Precision: 1-2 decimal places recommended

Example: 12.5 kg

Height / Length

Units: Centimeters (cm)

Note: Length (lying down) for infants <2 years; standing height for older children

Example: 85.3 cm

Head Circumference (OFC)

Units: Centimeters (cm)

Measurement: Maximum circumference measured around the widest part of the head

Example: 48.2 cm

Body Mass Index (BMI)

Calculation: Automatically calculated from weight and height

Formula: BMI = weight(kg) / [height(m)]¬≤

Interpretation: Provides age-adjusted centiles for nutritional status

Understanding Results

Centiles

Centiles show where a measurement falls in relation to a reference population:

  • 50th centile: Median value - 50% of children are above, 50% below
  • 25th-75th centile: Normal range - includes ~50% of healthy children
  • 9th-91st centile: Wider normal range - includes ~82% of children
  • <0.4th or >99.6th: Extreme values requiring clinical review

Standard Deviation Scores (SDS / Z-scores)

SDS values express how many standard deviations a measurement is from the mean:

  • 0 SDS: Exactly at the mean (50th centile)
  • +1 SDS: ~84th centile
  • +2 SDS: ~98th centile
  • -1 SDS: ~16th centile
  • -2 SDS: ~2nd centile

Values beyond ¬±2 SDS warrant further investigation.

The calculator provides warnings for extreme SDS values:

  • ¬±4 to ¬±8 SDS: Advisory warning - verify measurement accuracy
  • >¬±8 SDS: Hard rejection - measurement likely erroneous
  • BMI ¬±15 SDS: Higher threshold due to BMI variability

Preterm Infants

Gestational Age Correction

For babies born preterm (<37 weeks gestation), measurements are automatically corrected based on their expected due date (EDD):

Correction Duration:

  • 32-36 weeks gestation: Correction until age 1 year
  • <32 weeks gestation: Correction until age 2 years

How It Works:

The calculator adjusts the child's age based on their due date rather than birth date. Both chronological and corrected ages are displayed in results.

Example:

Baby born: 30 weeks gestation (10 weeks early)

Chronological age at 6 months: 6 months

Corrected age: 3.7 months (6 months - 10 weeks)

Measurements plotted at: 3.7 months on growth charts

Advanced Features

Height Velocity

Calculate yearly growth rate when previous height measurements are available.

Requirements:

  • Minimum interval: 4 months between measurements
  • Both current and previous heights required
  • Previous measurement date must be before current date

Clinical Use: Monitor growth patterns, assess growth hormone therapy, identify growth faltering.

Mid-Parental Height (MPH)

Predict adult height based on parental heights.

Calculation:

  • Boys: [(Mother's height + Father's height) / 2] + 6.5 cm
  • Girls: [(Mother's height + Father's height) / 2] - 6.5 cm

Target Range: MPH ¬± 8.5 cm (approximately ¬±2 SDS)

Interpretation: Most children reach adult height within this range. Values outside may indicate pathology.

Body Surface Area (BSA)

Used for medication dosing and clinical calculations.

Methods:

  • Boyd formula: Used when both weight and height available (most accurate)
  • cBNF lookup: Used when only weight available (based on British National Formulary tables)

Growth Hormone Dosing

Standard GH dose calculation: 7 mg/m¬≤/week

Displays:

  • Daily dose (mg/day)
  • Dose per body surface area (mg/m¬≤/week)
  • Weight-based dose (mcg/kg/day)

Note: This is a reference calculation. Actual GH doses should be prescribed by specialists based on individual patient needs.

BMI Percentage of Median

Shows BMI as percentage of the median (50th centile) value.

Clinical Interpretation:

  • <70%: Severe malnutrition
  • 70-80%: Moderate malnutrition
  • 80-90%: Mild malnutrition
  • 90-110%: Normal nutritional status
  • >120%: Overweight/obesity

Available in Advanced Mode only

Tips & Best Practices

üìè Accurate Measurements

  • Use calibrated equipment
  • Measure at consistent times of day (height can vary)
  • For length: ensure infant is lying straight, knees extended
  • For standing height: shoes off, heels against wall
  • OFC: measure at widest circumference (glabella to occiput)

üéØ Choosing the Right Reference

  • Use UK-WHO for general population
  • Use syndrome-specific charts only for confirmed diagnoses
  • Don't switch references unnecessarily - use consistently for serial measurements

üìä Interpreting Charts

  • Growth pattern (trajectory) is more important than single measurements
  • Crossing centiles up or down may indicate pathology
  • Consider parental heights - genetic potential affects growth
  • Use corrected age for preterm infants as appropriate
